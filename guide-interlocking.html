Interlocking Two Coils

Wire two coils so they can never both be energized at once — the safety foundation for forward/reverse.

Difficulty: IntermediateBuilds on: start/stopLesson: read the theory →

What you’re building

Parts to add

  • 1Power Source
  • 2Push Button NO
  • 2Coil
  • 2NC Contact

Steps

  1. Add and label

    Label one Coil F and the other R. Label one NC Contact F and the other R.

  2. Build rung F through R’s NC contact

    Power (L1) → Button 1 → NC Contact (R) → Coil F A1
    Coil F A2 → Power (N)

  3. Build rung R through F’s NC contact

    Power (L1) → Button 2 → NC Contact (F) → Coil R A1
    Coil R A2 → Power (N)

  4. Energize and try to break it

    Energize, then try to turn both coils on at once.

✓ Test it

  • Press button 1: coil F energizes. Now press button 2: coil R is LOCKED OUT (F’s NC contact opened its path).
  • Release F, then press button 2: now R can energize. They take turns — never both.
Tip: This is exactly the interlock used in forward/reverse. The first coil in vetoes the other through its NC contact.
